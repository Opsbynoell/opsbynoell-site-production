## Permanent rules
- Do not redesign from scratch. Use the actual Schedule Marketing Template source in `docs/template-source/schedule-marketing-template/` as the structural foundation.
- Keep the site inside one authored world.
- No coral.
- Warm neutrals, blush/pink family, soft lilac, and wine/oxblood accents.
- Purple is reserved only for Nova-specific moments.
- Preserve full architecture: Home, Systems, Verticals, Pricing, Nova, About, Book.
- Use `Verticals` as the architecture and route system.
- Keep the homepage psychology sequence: Recognition → Tension → Proof → Relief → Action.
- Preserve urgency, pressure, proof, and conversion.
- Keep proof tangible and operational.
- `/book` must be a real booking experience, not a stub.
- `/nova` must be a real page, not a stub.
- Nova chat must be visible and testable in preview.

## Design discipline
- Do not let the build drift into generic startup-template aesthetics.
- Do not stack trendy components.
- Premium framed proof objects are allowed if they feel authored.
- Motion is enhancement only, never a dependency for visibility.
- If the logo disappeared, the site should still feel unmistakably like Ops by Noell.

## Working test
Before calling a pass done, ask:
- Does this feel like the moodboard made it into the product?
- Is the communication-then / AI-now bridge visible?
- Does proof feel operational and real?
- Are `/book`, `/nova`, and Nova chat genuinely usable and believable?
